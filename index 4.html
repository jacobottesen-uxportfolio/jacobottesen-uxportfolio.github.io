This is me

UX Research @Workfront

I am currently a senior at BYU-Idaho (Online) pursuing a BS in Web Design & Development and living in Salt Lake City. When I am not doing UX work, you will find me eating Trader Joe's cookie butter, hiking, doing photography, camping, or growing a mustache

I started college as a pre-physical therapy major due to multiple career assessments I took. After joining the entrepreneurship society on campus, I learned a new term called “UX design.” I researched the crap out of it and fell in love with the iterative process and methodologies of UX.

Soon after that, I changed my degree to web design & development. Since then it has been a roller coaster of reading blogs, personal projects, internships, and design conferences.

Currently, I work as a UX designer for the university I attend, working on redesigning a tool used by 18,000+ students to help them plan out classes for graduation. Previously, I interned at Workfront as a UX research/design intern. There, I learned about the importance of understanding how design fits into the business. Before that I interned at Boncom, a design agency focused on non-profits in Salt Lake City.

I am looking for future opportunities to grow.
