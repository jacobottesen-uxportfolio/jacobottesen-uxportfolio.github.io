Grad Planner Redesign

Project Scope:

  • My Role: UX Design, Research
  • Tools: Adobe XD, Paper & Pen
  • Timeline: Sep - Dec 2018
  • Deliverables: Sketches, interactive XD prototype, research findings
  • Team: Me, Vlad Balashoff, Nathan Jones

Introduction:

Grad Planner is a web application used as a platform for students to plan out their required courses for graduation. The tool also connects students to academic advisors from the school.

Students use Grad Planner to view required courses, plan courses by semester, declare their major, change their major, and monitor graduation progress.

The Problem:

Students are required to perform essential tasks for graduation on the Grad Planner platform, yet the current interface is painful to use. The web app leaves students feeling confused and frustrated. As a result, the academic advising office is overloaded with student appointments and questions because they are unable to effectively use the tool.

Current UI of Grad Planner

Initial Research

User Interviews

I interviewed 18 students to explore who our users were, how they were using Grad Planner, and how their experience had been overall. During the interviews, I asked questions like “Tell me about the last time you used Grad Planner?”,“Why did you last use it?”,“How does Grad Planner help you as a student?" Here are some key insights I gained:

Lack of Feature Awareness

Majority of users were unaware of the features on Grad Planner such as changing a major, creating an alternate plan, and comparing plans.

Aesthetics and Feel

Almost all the students mentioned that Grad Planner’s UI was outdated, interactions felt awkward and had a slow loading time.

Overwhelming Interface

Many students, but particularly freshman, noted feeling overwhelmed by the amount of information presented in the dashboard.

Usability Testing

As a team, we conducted usability tests with 10 participants. This testing allowed us to dig deeper and discover more issues with the interface. Here are a few key pain points:

Pain Point #1

Students struggled to complete the task of changing their major due to the selection of "create an alternate plan" being hidden in a dropdown menu.

Pain Point #2

Dashboard contains confusing and irrelevant information for students:

Pain Point #3

Students have no way to find required courses for their degree without first opening an accordion, followed by selecting a course placeholder, which finally displays required courses:

Ideate

After gaining a solid understanding of the pain points in Grad Planner, we set up to redesign the whole interface with a focus on aiding student success. Although, we found many pain points, in this case study I will only address the changing a major/creating an alternate plan to help demonstrate my design process.

What is an alternate plan?
An alternate plan is a “sandbox” plan that allows a student to explore the scenario of another major without changing their current plan.

HMW Question:

How might we streamline crucial information and features that aid student success?

Design Goals

Update

Bring site aesthetics up to speed with current visual trends and patterns.

Feature Awareness

Bring awareness of important features to students.

Relevant Information

Provide students with necessary information about their planned graduation.

Case Study In-Progress

Current Lessons Learned:

  • Don't design alone. Design in the open. We, as a team, didn't work close enough with the developers so when it came to passing off our design work, there were many changes that needed to be made.
  • Conduct an analysis as team. Coming together as team after conducting research helped us to form a consensus as to what our next steps should be.
